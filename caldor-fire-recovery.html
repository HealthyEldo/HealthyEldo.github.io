CALDOR FIRE RECOVERY

Restoring forest resilience and community recovery after the devastating Caldor Fire through the Healthy Eldorado Landscape Partnership.

Learn More

Project Overview

The Caldor Fire of 2021 burned over 221,000 acres across the Eldorado National Forest and surrounding areas, creating an urgent need for comprehensive recovery and restoration efforts. The HELP Caldor Fire Recovery project focuses on:

Immediate Recovery

Emergency stabilization, erosion control, and watershed protection to prevent further environmental damage.

Long-term Restoration

Strategic reforestation, habitat restoration, and community infrastructure rebuilding for sustainable recovery.

Get Involved

Recovery Goals

Forest Restoration

Replanting native species and restoring forest ecosystems for long-term resilience.

Watershed Protection

Implementing erosion control and water quality protection measures.

Community Recovery

Supporting local communities in rebuilding infrastructure and livelihoods.

Fire Resilience

Building landscapes that are more resilient to future wildfire events.
